# Article to Presentation Structure Skill

## Purpose

This skill helps transform a long-form article, report, essay, transcript, or white paper into a presentation-ready structure.

The goal is not to summarize the article paragraph by paragraph, but to extract its argument, logic, evidence, and implications, then reorganize them into a slide-based narrative.

## Input

The user may provide:

- A full article
- A URL or pasted text
- A report, paper, blog post, news article, transcript, or internal memo
- Optional audience information
- Optional presentation length
- Optional purpose, such as teaching, briefing, sales, proposal, executive summary, or training

## Core Process

### 1. Identify the presentation context

Infer or ask for:

- Target audience
- Presentation goal
- Expected duration
- Desired depth
- Tone
- Whether the output should be educational, persuasive, analytical, executive-oriented, or instructional

If missing, make reasonable assumptions and state them.

### 2. Extract the article structure

Analyze the article for:

- Main thesis
- Core arguments
- Supporting evidence
- Important examples
- Definitions
- Data points
- Causes and effects
- Problems and solutions
- Contrasts or trade-offs
- Conclusions or recommendations

Do not simply follow the article’s original order if another structure is better for presentation.

### 3. Determine the narrative arc

Choose the most suitable presentation flow, such as:

- Problem → Cause → Solution → Action
- Context → Insight → Implication → Recommendation
- What → Why → How → Example → Takeaway
- Before → Change → After
- Claim → Evidence → Counterpoint → Conclusion
- Situation → Complication → Resolution
- Concept → Framework → Application → Practice

### 4. Create slide-level structure

For each slide, produce:

- Slide number
- Slide title
- Main message
- Key bullet points
- Suggested visual type
- Speaker note or explanation
- Source material from the article, if useful

Avoid vague slide titles like “Overview” or “Background.” Use message-driven titles.

Bad:
- Market Trends

Good:
- AI adoption is accelerating fastest in operational workflows

### 5. Separate content density by slide type

Use different slide types when appropriate:

- Title slide
- Agenda slide
- Executive summary
- Problem framing
- Key insight slide
- Data/chart slide
- Framework slide
- Comparison slide
- Timeline slide
- Process slide
- Case/example slide
- Recommendation slide
- Risk/limitation slide
- Closing takeaway slide

### 6. Identify what should not be included

Remove:

- Repetitive details
- Overly technical explanations unless audience requires them
- Minor examples
- Weak evidence
- Tangential arguments
- Article-specific prose that does not help the presentation

### 7. Produce final output

The final output should include:

1. Presentation objective
2. Assumed audience
3. One-sentence core message
4. Recommended narrative structure
5. Slide-by-slide outline
6. Optional shortened version
7. Optional expanded version
8. Notes on what was omitted and why
